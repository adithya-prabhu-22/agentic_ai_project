COMPLETE INCOME TAX GUIDE INDIA FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)
NEW TAX REGIME SLABS

Up to 4,00,000 – 0%
4,00,001 to 8,00,000 – 5%
8,00,001 to 12,00,000 – 10%
12,00,001 to 16,00,000 – 15%
16,00,001 to 20,00,000 – 20%
20,00,001 to 24,00,000 – 25%
Above 24,00,000 – 30%

OLD TAX REGIME SLABS

Up to 2,50,000 – 0%
2,50,001 to 5,00,000 – 5%
5,00,001 to 10,00,000 – 20%
Above 10,00,000 – 30%

SECTION 87A REBATE

New Regime:
Maximum rebate: 60,000
Eligible if total income ≤ 12,00,000
Tax-free income effectively up to 12,00,000 (subject to rebate limit)

Old Regime:
Maximum rebate: 12,500
Eligible if total income ≤ 5,00,000

STANDARD DEDUCTION

Old Regime:
Standard deduction: 50,000
Family pension deduction: 50,000

New Regime:
Standard deduction: 75,000
Family pension deduction: 25,000

DEDUCTIONS UNDER OLD REGIME

Section 80C:
Maximum limit 1,50,000
Includes:
Life insurance premium
PPF
EPF
ELSS
NSC
5-year fixed deposits
Senior citizens savings scheme
Sukanya Samriddhi
Tuition fees (max 2 children)
Home loan principal
Stamp duty and registration
ULIP
Pension plans

Section 80CCD:
80CCD(1): NPS contribution within 80C limit
80CCD(1B): Additional NPS deduction 50,000
80CCD(2): Employer NPS contribution up to 10% (14% for Central Govt)

Section 80D:
Self, spouse, children: 25,000
Parents below 60: 25,000
Senior citizen parents: 50,000
Maximum possible: 1,00,000

Section 80DD:
Normal disability: 75,000
Severe disability: 1,25,000

Section 80DDB:
Below 60: 40,000
60 to 80 years: 1,00,000
Above 80 years: 1,25,000

Section 80E:
Education loan interest
No upper limit
Maximum 8 years

Section 80EE:
Home loan interest additional 50,000
Specific loan and property limits apply

Section 80EEA:
Affordable housing
Additional deduction 1,50,000

Section 80G:
Donations eligible 50% or 100% deduction

Section 80TTA:
Savings interest up to 10,000

Section 80TTB:
Senior citizens interest deduction up to 50,000

HOME LOAN BENEFITS

Section 24(b):
Old regime self-occupied property: 2,00,000 interest
New regime: not allowed for self-occupied
Let-out property: full interest allowed

Section 80EEB:
Electric vehicle loan interest up to 1,50,000

HRA

Old Regime:
Exemption is minimum of:
Actual HRA
Rent minus 10% salary
50% salary (metro) or 40% (non-metro)

New Regime:
No HRA exemption

CAPITAL GAINS

Long Term Capital Gains:
Immovable property holding >24 months
Listed shares holding >12 months
Rate generally 20% with indexation or 10% without indexation
Equity LTCG above 1,00,000 taxed at 10%

Short Term Capital Gains:
Equity under section 111A: 15%
Others: taxed as per slab

INTEREST PROVISIONS

Section 234A:
1% per month for late filing

Section 234B:
1% per month if advance tax paid < 90%

1% per month for deferment

SURCHARGE

Above 50 lakhs – 10%
Above 1 crore – 15%
Above 2 crores – 25%
Above 5 crores – 37%

HEALTH AND EDUCATION CESS

4% on income tax plus surcharge

WHEN TO CHOOSE OLD REGIME

If claiming multiple deductions
If home loan interest ≥ 2,00,000
If claiming HRA
If large 80C and 80D investments

WHEN TO CHOOSE NEW REGIME

If minimal deductions
If income between 7–15 lakhs
If preferring simpler structure
If no HRA or home loan
